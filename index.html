Frequently asked questions

Common questions.

I received a court notice. What should I do first?

Identify the county, court, date, time, type of case and documents you received. If you have a docket number, agency notice or PennDOT letter, have it available when contacting the office.

How do I know which practice area fits my issue?

Start with the document or event that created the problem. Arrest documents, family-court orders, agency notices, insurance letters and contract disputes usually point to the right starting place.

Can I send confidential facts right away?

No. Start with scheduling information and a short description. Save confidential details until the office confirms that the issue can be discussed.

What should I have ready?

Bring court documents, citations, orders, notices, contracts, photos, messages, medical records, insurance letters, income information, employment records and any letters with dates.

Does contacting the office create an attorney-client relationship?

No. An attorney-client relationship is created only after conflict check, acceptance of representation and completion of engagement terms.

Can anyone promise a specific result?

No. Legal outcomes depend on the facts, law, procedure, evidence, the court or agency involved and decisions made by courts, agencies, opposing parties or insurers.
