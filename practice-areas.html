Finding the right starting point

Start with what happened, then choose the path.

You do not need to know the perfect practice-area label before contacting the office. Start with the event or paper that triggered the problem and the date, if any, that is already on the calendar.

An arrest may require court and bail review. A custody dispute may turn on an existing order and the child's routine. A UC hearing may depend on the determination, exhibits and witnesses. An injury or employment issue may start with reports, records, agency notices or insurance letters.

Use the pages below to choose the closest fit. If the issue crosses categories, say so when you contact the office and have the paper, date and county ready.

How to prepare

Useful information to have ready.

Good preparation starts with what arrived, what happened and what date is next. Court documents, notices, orders, citations, contracts, photographs, messages, medical records, insurance letters and agency notices can change the first conversation.

When contacting the office, be ready to identify the county, names of involved parties and whether any hearing, arraignment, conference, appeal or response date is already scheduled. For Northampton County matters, include whether the documents come from the Court of Common Pleas, a magisterial district court, Domestic Relations, PennDOT, unemployment compensation or another agency.

For urgent matters

Contact the office promptly rather than relying on a short message alone. Deadlines and court appearances should be addressed promptly.
