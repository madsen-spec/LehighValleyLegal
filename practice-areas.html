Most people do not start by knowing the legal category. They focus on a charge, a DUI stop, a custody problem, a workplace matter, an injury, a property dispute, an agency notice or a court case. This page helps you choose the closest starting point.

  • What happened and where things stand now.
  • The county, court, agency or municipality.
  • Any hearing, appeal, filing or response date.

Finding the right starting point

Focus on what happened, then choose the path.

You do not need to know the perfect practice-area label before contacting our office. Focus on what happened, who is involved, where the matter is pending and whether anything is scheduled.

Different problems call for different first steps. A criminal charge may require attention to bail or a hearing. A custody problem may turn on a current order and the child's routine. A workplace or injury matter may depend on timing, proof and communications already received.

Use the pages below to choose the closest fit. If the matter crosses categories, say so on the first call and identify the county and any scheduled event.

Before contacting our office

Basic facts that help at the start.

The first questions are usually simple: what happened, where is the matter pending, who is involved and is anything already scheduled? The answer may come from a court notice, agency letter, order, citation, contract, photo, message, insurance letter or another record.

For Northampton County matters, it helps to identify whether the matter is in the Court of Common Pleas, a magisterial district court, Domestic Relations, PennDOT, unemployment compensation or another agency.

For urgent matters

Use the direct line promptly rather than relying on a short message alone. Deadlines and court appearances should be addressed promptly.

Discuss the next step

Not sure where it fits?

Briefly describe the problem, county and any scheduled date. We can tell you what kind of information is appropriate for the first step.
