Practice areas

Find the right Easton legal starting point.

Most people do not start by knowing the legal category. They focus on a charge, a DUI stop, a custody problem, a workplace issue, an injury, a property dispute, an agency notice or a court case. This page helps you choose the closest starting point.

What helps us start

  • What happened and where things stand now.
  • The county, court, agency or municipality.
  • Any hearing, appeal, filing or response date.

Finding the right starting point

Focus on what happened, then choose the path.

You do not need to know the perfect practice-area label before contacting the office. Focus on what happened, who is involved, where the issue is pending and whether anything is scheduled.

Different problems call for different first steps. A criminal charge may require attention to bail or a hearing. A custody problem may turn on a current order and the child's routine. A workplace or injury issue may depend on timing, proof and communications already received.

Use the pages below to choose the closest fit. If the issue crosses categories, say so when you contact the office and identify the county and any scheduled event.

Before contacting the office

Basic facts that help at the start.

The first questions are usually simple: what happened, where is the issue pending, who is involved and is anything already scheduled? The answer may come from a court notice, agency letter, order, citation, contract, photo, message, insurance letter or another record.

For Northampton County matters, it helps to identify whether the issue is in the Court of Common Pleas, a magisterial district court, Domestic Relations, PennDOT, unemployment compensation or another agency.

For urgent matters

Contact the office promptly rather than relying on a short message alone. Deadlines and court appearances should be addressed promptly.
