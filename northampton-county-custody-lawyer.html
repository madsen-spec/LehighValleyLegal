Local preparation

The current custody order is the starting point.

Custody disputes can involve legal custody, physical custody, holiday schedules, exchanges, relocation, contempt, school choice, medical decisions and communication between parents. The current order usually frames what can be enforced or changed.

For Northampton County matters, identify whether a conference, hearing, petition, emergency filing, contempt issue or relocation notice is pending. The next date and the requested relief should be clear before background details are discussed.

What matters first

What to gather before the first call.

Order

Use the current order

Bring the signed custody order, any modifications and any pending petition or conference notice.

Schedule

Make the routine visible

A calendar showing school, exchanges, childcare, work schedules and missed time can clarify the dispute.

Evidence

Save dated examples

Keep messages, emails, school papers, medical information and travel details with dates where possible.

Next steps

Custody preparation should be specific, not generic.

A list of broad complaints is less useful than dated examples tied to the order. Organize what happened, when it happened, who was present and how it affected the child or schedule.

Do not send confidential child-related details in an initial message. Start with the parties, county, current order, next date and general issue type for conflict checking.

Court-specific forms and instructions

Northampton County family-court forms and self-help materials are court-specific. Confirm whether the current issue involves a new filing, modification, contempt, relocation, or another procedure.

Court staff limits

Self-help materials may explain forms and procedure, but court staff cannot give legal advice. Keep questions about legal strategy for an attorney after conflict check.

Questions

Common preparation questions.

What if there is no custody order yet?

Identify whether any filing, conference notice or informal schedule exists, and gather school, childcare and parent-contact information.

What if the other parent is not following the order?

Bring the order and dated examples showing what happened, including exchange messages, calendars and missed-time records.

Can custody overlap with PFA or divorce?

Yes. Bring related PFA, divorce, support or criminal papers because separate proceedings may affect timing and options.
