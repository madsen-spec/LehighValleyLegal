Specific examples are better than broad complaints.

A list of broad complaints is less helpful than examples tied to the order: what happened, when it happened, who was present and how it affected the child or schedule.

Northampton County custody

Write for the conference officer, not just for the other parent.

The search question here is practical: what happens in the Northampton County custody process and how should a parent prepare?

James's view: A custody petition should ask for a schedule the court can actually order and supervise. Vague complaints do not carry the same weight as dated examples.

What controls the next step

The custody analysis is built around 23 Pa.C.S.A. § 5328, and relocation requires 23 Pa.C.S.A. § 5337 notice and analysis.

Northampton County custody conferences usually begin with a conference officer and can escalate to a judge if the case does not resolve.

Next: Bring the existing order, school calendar, proposed schedule and three concrete examples of the problem.

Custody questions

Custody questions.

What if there is no custody order yet?

Say whether anything has been filed and describe the child's current routine, school, childcare and parent-contact situation.

What if the other parent is not following the order?

Use the order and dated examples to show what happened, including exchange messages, calendars and missed time.

Can custody overlap with PFA or divorce?

Yes. Related PFA, divorce, support or criminal matters may affect timing, procedure and available options.
