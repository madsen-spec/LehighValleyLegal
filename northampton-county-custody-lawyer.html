Order

Know the current order

The signed order, any modifications and any pending petition show the court posture.

Schedule

Make the routine visible

School, exchanges, childcare, work schedules and missed time often explain the real dispute better than labels.

Proof

Use dated examples

Messages, emails, calendars, school information and medical details can show what actually happened.

What happens next

Specific examples are better than broad complaints.

A list of broad complaints is less helpful than examples tied to the order: what happened, when it happened, who was present and how it affected the child or schedule.

Do not send confidential child-related details in an initial message. For scheduling, identify the parties, county, current order and general custody issue.

Court-specific forms and instructions

Northampton County family-court forms and self-help materials are court-specific. Confirm whether the current issue involves a new filing, modification, contempt, relocation, or another procedure.

Court staff limits

Self-help materials may explain forms and procedure, but court staff cannot give legal advice. Strategy depends on the facts, the order and the relief requested.

Custody questions

Custody questions.

What if there is no custody order yet?

Say whether anything has been filed and describe the child's current routine, school, childcare and parent-contact situation.

What if the other parent is not following the order?

Use the order and dated examples to show what happened, including exchange messages, calendars and missed time.

Can custody overlap with PFA or divorce?

Yes. Related PFA, divorce, support or criminal matters may affect timing and options.
