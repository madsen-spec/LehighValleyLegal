PennDOT license issues

License questions may involve suspension notices, restoration letters, limited license applications, interlock requirements and appeal periods. Keep envelopes, letter dates and proof of mailing when possible.

Traffic citations and summary appeals

A citation can affect points, insurance, license status, employment and CDL consequences. Summary appeals and magisterial district court hearings have response dates, so review should happen before the response date passes.

Commercial and employment concerns

Drivers who need a license for work should raise CDL, company vehicle, rideshare, delivery, school, military or professional licensing concerns during the first conversation. A traffic disposition that seems minor can still affect work-related driving.

DUI and traffic FAQ

Common questions.

Is a PennDOT letter separate from the criminal case?

Often, yes. A court case and PennDOT license issue may have different steps and deadlines. Bring both court documents and PennDOT notices so the license and criminal timelines can be evaluated together.

What matters after a DUI arrest?

The complaint, citations, bail terms, testing or refusal issue, license notices, hearing notices and any ARD, treatment or interlock information may matter. If you received court or PennDOT mail, keep the envelopes.

Can a traffic ticket affect my license?

Some traffic matters can affect points, suspensions, insurance, employment and CDL status. Evaluate the citation before deciding how to respond, especially if you already have points or prior suspensions.

What if I already received a suspension notice?

Act promptly and identify the date on the notice, the reason listed and any restoration or appeal information included with the letter. A suspension notice should not be ignored because the license issue may continue even while the court matter is pending.
