PennDOT license matters

License questions may involve suspension notices, restoration letters, limited license applications, interlock requirements and appeal periods. Keep envelopes, letter dates and proof of mailing when possible.

Traffic citations and summary appeals

Commercial and employment concerns

DUI and traffic FAQ

Common questions.

Is a PennDOT letter separate from the criminal case?

Often, yes. A court case and PennDOT license matter may have different steps and timing. Keep both court documents and PennDOT notices so the license and criminal timelines can be evaluated together.

What matters after a DUI arrest?

The complaint, citations, bail terms, testing or refusal matter, license notices, hearing notices and any ARD, treatment or interlock information may matter. If you received court or PennDOT mail, keep the envelopes.

Can a traffic ticket affect my license?

Some traffic matters may affect points, suspensions, insurance, employment or CDL status. Review the citation before deciding how to respond, especially if you already have points or prior suspensions.

What if I already received a suspension notice?

Act promptly and identify the date on the notice, the reason listed and any restoration or appeal information included with the letter. A suspension notice should not be ignored because the license matter may continue even while the court matter is pending.
