Claim record

Do not let the record depend on memory alone.

Accident evidence can become harder to gather with time. Photos are deleted, witnesses move, security video may be overwritten and treatment details become harder to reconstruct. A short written timeline and organized records help an attorney evaluate liability and damages more efficiently.

For Easton-area vehicle crashes, the record may include the police report, photos of the vehicles and scene, insurance information, treatment records and repair estimates. For falls or other property-related injuries, photos of the condition, incident reports, store or property communications and witness information may matter.

Pennsylvania's two-year limitation statute often applies to personal injury claims, but there may be exceptions, shorter notice requirements or fact-specific matters. The actual filing date should be checked early, especially when a public entity, minor, delayed diagnosis or unusual claim is involved.

An early legal evaluation should also consider whether the available insurance information is complete. Health insurance, auto coverage, medical payments coverage, liability coverage, uninsured or underinsured motorist coverage, workers' compensation overlap and liens may affect practical claim decisions.

Insurance papers matter.

Save every letter, message, text and form from an insurer. Do not sign a release, settlement check, broad medical authorization or recorded statement request without understanding what rights or records it affects.

Local accident matters

Common Easton injury questions.

Each claim is different, but most early conversations need the same core information: what happened, what proof exists, what treatment occurred and what insurance or timing matter may apply.

Vehicle crashes

Preserve the report number, photos, driver and insurance information, medical records, repair estimates and any letters from insurers. If the crash involved a rideshare, commercial vehicle, pedestrian, motorcycle, bicycle or uninsured driver, identify that immediately.

Premises injuries

Photographs, incident reports, weather or maintenance details, witness names and communications with the property owner may be important.

Medical treatment timeline

List providers, dates, referrals, prescriptions, restrictions and symptoms. Keep records showing missed work or activity limitations. Follow medical guidance from treating providers and keep copies of discharge instructions and referral documents.

Claim communication

Adjusters may ask for statements, forms or releases. Preserve those requests and ask questions before signing documents you do not understand. Keep a log of calls, messages and letters.

Easton injury FAQ

Common questions.

What if I do not have the police report yet?

Prepare the crash date, location, department, incident number and officer information if available. The report can often be obtained later, but photos, insurance communications and treatment records should still be preserved.

What if the insurance company already contacted me?

Save the message, claim number and adjuster contact information. Ask what is being requested before giving a recorded statement or signing a release. Do not rely on a phone call as the only record of what was discussed.

Should I keep seeing my doctors?

Follow medical advice from your treating providers. From a claim perspective, gaps, referrals, restrictions and treatment records can all affect how damages are documented.

Does every injury claim go to court?

No. Some claims are handled through insurance negotiations, and others may require litigation. The path depends on liability, damages, coverage, deadlines and the response from the other side.
