What controls

A Lehigh County criminal case has a procedural path.

Many cases begin before a magisterial district judge and may proceed to the Lehigh County Court of Common Pleas. The documents—not a guess based on the arrest location—identifies the controlling court and date.

Do not miss court, violate bail conditions or make additional statements without understanding the consequences.

Do not wait on a listed date

If a paper lists a hearing, response, appeal or license date, use that date as the starting point.

An early review should connect the record to the practical risk.

Charges, evidence, prior record, bail, probation status and collateral effects can change the strategy. Employment, custody, immigration or firearms concerns should be identified early without assuming the criminal case is the only issue.
