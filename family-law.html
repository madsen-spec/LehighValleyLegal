Custody and family preparation

Specific facts are stronger than general concerns.

For custody and support matters, bring the current schedule, existing orders, school and medical information, income records, expense documents and communications that show dates and context. A clear timeline helps separate urgent issues from long-term parenting or financial disputes.

In Northampton County family matters, the first evaluation should identify any current order, conference date, hearing notice, PFA deadline, support conference, filing date and facts that affect the child's safety, school routine, medical needs, transportation or communication with each parent.

General concerns are less useful than dated examples. Instead of only saying communication is poor, identify the dates, messages and practical effect. Instead of only saying the schedule does not work, identify school, work, transportation, childcare or safety facts that explain why a different order is being requested.

Custody and parenting time

Custody issues usually require a specific schedule, facts about the child and a clear explanation of why the requested arrangement fits the child's needs and the governing legal factors. Bring the current order, actual schedule and proposed schedule.

Support and divorce

Support and divorce issues depend on income, expenses, assets, debts and timing. Accurate financial records help avoid preventable delay. Bring pay stubs, tax returns, account statements, mortgage or rent information, insurance details and debt records.

PFA and contempt matters

Protection and contempt issues often move quickly. Bring the order, petition, service papers, hearing date, messages, photos, police reports, medical records and witness information.

Service and filing details

Custody and divorce papers must be filed and served under the applicable rules. Be ready to identify what has been filed, who was served and what proof of service exists.

Family law FAQ

Common questions.

What should I bring for a custody consultation?

Bring existing orders, the current schedule, hearing notices, school and medical information, messages, transportation details, childcare details and a practical schedule proposal. A short timeline with dates is helpful.

What if a PFA hearing is coming up?

Act promptly and identify the hearing date, county, order terms, allegations, police involvement and any documents or messages tied to the incident. Bring the temporary order, petition, service papers and any related criminal documents.

Do I need income documents for support or divorce?

Yes. Bring pay stubs, tax returns, benefit information, expenses, debts, account information and any prior support or divorce orders. Financial records often control the advice more than general estimates.

Can I change an existing custody order?

Modification depends on the current order, facts, procedure and timing. Bring the order and dated examples explaining what changed. A proposed schedule is also helpful because the court usually needs to know what arrangement is being requested.
