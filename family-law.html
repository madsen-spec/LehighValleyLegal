Custody and family issues

Specific facts are stronger than general concerns.

For custody and support matters, the current schedule, existing orders, school and medical information, income, expenses and dated communications can shape the first discussion. A clear timeline helps separate urgent issues from long-term parenting or financial disputes.

In Northampton County family matters, the first evaluation should identify any current order, conference date, hearing notice, PFA deadline, support conference, filing date and facts that affect the child's safety, school routine, medical needs, transportation or communication with each parent.

Dated examples are stronger than broad complaints. Instead of only saying communication is poor, identify the dates, messages and practical effect. Instead of only saying the schedule does not work, identify the school, work, transportation, childcare or safety facts behind the request.

Custody and parenting time

Custody issues often require a specific schedule, facts about the child and a clear explanation of why the requested arrangement fits the child's needs and the governing legal factors. The current order, actual schedule and proposed schedule should be part of that discussion.

Support and divorce

Support and divorce issues depend on income, expenses, assets, debts and timing. Accurate financial information can prevent avoidable delay.

PFA and contempt matters

Protection and contempt issues often move quickly. The order, petition, service issue, hearing date, messages, photos, police reports, medical information and witness information may all matter.

Service and filing details

Custody and divorce papers must be filed and served under the applicable rules. Be ready to identify what has been filed, who was served and what proof of service exists.

Family law FAQ

Common questions.

What matters for a custody consultation?

Existing orders, the current schedule, hearing notices, school and medical information, messages, transportation details, childcare details and a practical schedule proposal may all matter. A short timeline with dates can help.

What if a PFA hearing is coming up?

Act promptly and identify the hearing date, county, order terms, allegations, police involvement and any messages, photos or related court issues tied to the incident.

Do I need income documents for support or divorce?

Yes. Pay stubs, tax returns, benefit information, expenses, debts, account information and any prior support or divorce orders may matter. Financial facts often matter more than rough estimates.

Can I change an existing custody order?

Modification depends on the current order, facts, procedure and timing. Dated examples explaining what changed and a proposed schedule can help focus the request.
