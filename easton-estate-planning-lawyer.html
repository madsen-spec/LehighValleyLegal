What matters first

What to gather before the first call.

Documents

Existing planning papers

Bring wills, trusts, powers of attorney, health-care directives and prior estate documents.

People

Decision-makers and beneficiaries

List possible executors, agents, guardians, trustees and beneficiaries with contact information when available.

Property

Assets and beneficiary forms

Gather deeds, account types, vehicle titles, insurance information and beneficiary forms.

Next steps

Avoid guessing about ownership and beneficiaries.

How property is titled and whether an account has a beneficiary designation can change what a will controls. Bring the actual documents when possible.

Document choices depend on facts

Wills, powers of attorney, health-care directions, trusts, and beneficiary designations serve different purposes. Do not assume one document covers every asset or decision.

Ownership and beneficiary details

Before drafting, confirm real-estate titles, account ownership, beneficiary forms, insurance documents and family relationships from current records.

Questions

Common preparation questions.

What should I gather before discussing a will?

Gather family names, property information, existing documents, beneficiary forms and possible fiduciary choices.

Do beneficiary forms matter?

Yes. Beneficiary forms may control certain accounts or policies, so they should be reviewed with the rest of the plan.

Can this page tell me which documents I need?

No. It helps organize information. Actual document choices depend on facts and legal advice.
