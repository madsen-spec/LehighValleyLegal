Easton estate planning

Plan for incapacity and death in the same conversation.

A will, durable power of attorney, health-care power of attorney, living will and revocable trust do different jobs.

James's view: Do not choose a trust because it sounds sophisticated. Choose the document that solves the actual family, tax or property problem.

What controls the next step

Pennsylvania inheritance-tax rates are commonly described as 0 percent for a spouse, 4.5 percent for lineal heirs, 12 percent for siblings and 15 percent for other beneficiaries.

Easton estate administration often starts with the Northampton County Register of Wills and property records.

Next: Bring family names, addresses, fiduciary choices, deeds, account information and any existing estate-planning documents.

Planning questions

Estate-planning questions.

What should I think about before discussing a will?

Think about who should receive property, who should handle responsibilities and whether any old documents or beneficiary forms need attention.

Do beneficiary forms matter?

Yes. Beneficiary forms may control certain accounts or policies, so they should be reviewed with the rest of the plan.

Can this page tell me which documents I need?

No. It explains what information may matter. Actual document choices depend on facts and legal advice.
