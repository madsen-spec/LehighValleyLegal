This page provides general information. It does not provide safety advice or legal advice for any specific situation. If there is immediate danger, use emergency resources. For legal questions about hearing dates, service issues, alleged violations or related criminal or custody matters, contact the office directly.

A first discussion should focus on the exact order and petition, not just a general description of the dispute. The wording of the order can affect contact, residence, custody exchange, school or childcare communication, property access, firearms restrictions and what conduct might create a separate contempt or criminal issue.

Information that may matter

  • temporary or final PFA orders, petitions, and hearing notices;
  • service papers, police reports, criminal complaints, or bail documents if any exist;
  • custody orders, parenting schedules, school or medical documents involving children;
  • messages, photos, call logs, medical records, witness names, and dated timelines;
  • any firearm, residence, property, employment, school, childcare, exchange-location or third-party-contact terms listed in the order;
  • any violation, contempt, arrest, bail, probation or criminal-case documents connected to the same events or parties.

Call-first situations

Contact the office when there is a hearing date, arrest, contempt allegation, custody overlap, firearm-related term, or uncertainty about what an order allows or prohibits. Do not rely on a short message alone for urgent PFA matters.

For petitioners

For petitioners, the order, petition, hearing notice, service information, police or medical information, relevant communications, child-related issues and a timeline of events may all matter. If the order involves residence, custody, school, childcare, firearms, property retrieval or third-party contact, identify each term that may need explanation or enforcement.

For respondents

For respondents, every page served, the hearing date, related criminal issues, custody or residence issues, communications and prior orders or pending cases may matter. Do not assume that informal contact, a text message, a third-party communication or an agreed change is safe without reviewing the actual order and any later court order.

Hearing issues

A PFA hearing may require focused testimony and clear exhibits. The petition, temporary order, proof of service, photos, messages, call logs, medical or police information, witness names and date-specific facts may all matter. Keep originals unchanged and preserve the full context of messages or images.

Custody and family-court overlap

PFA terms may affect custody exchanges, school communication, household access, transportation and related family-court orders. Identify any current custody, support or divorce order and any upcoming conference, hearing, exchange issue or safety concern tied to the same family matter.

Criminal or contempt overlap

If there is an arrest, criminal complaint, bail condition, probation issue, firearm-related term or alleged violation, say so. PFA, contempt and criminal cases can move on separate tracks, and the first discussion should identify every court date, judge, docket number and condition already in place.

First-call information

Have the county, hearing date, order type, service status, involved party names, related docket numbers and any urgent safety, custody, residence or criminal-case issue ready. Keep confidential facts out of the initial message until the office confirms that it can speak with you about the issue.
