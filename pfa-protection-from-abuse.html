This page provides general information. It does not provide safety advice or legal advice for any specific situation. If there is immediate danger, use emergency resources. Use the direct line for questions about hearing dates, service, alleged violations or related criminal or custody matters.

A first discussion should focus on the exact order and petition, not just a general description of the dispute. The wording of the order can affect contact, residence, custody exchange, school or childcare communication, property access, firearms restrictions and what conduct might create a separate contempt or criminal matter.

Information that may matter

  • temporary or final PFA orders, petitions, and hearing notices;
  • service papers, police reports, criminal complaints, or bail documents if any exist;
  • custody orders, parenting schedules, school or medical documents involving children;
  • messages, photos, call logs, medical records, witness names, and dated timelines;
  • any firearm, residence, property, employment, school, childcare, exchange-location or third-party-contact terms listed in the order;
  • any violation, contempt, arrest, bail, probation or criminal-case documents connected to the same events or parties.

Call-first situations

Contact James when there is a hearing date, arrest, contempt allegation, custody overlap, firearm-related term or uncertainty about what an order allows or prohibits. Do not rely on a short message alone for urgent PFA matters.

For petitioners

For petitioners, the order, petition, hearing notice, service information, police or medical information, relevant communications, child-related matters and a timeline of events may all matter. If the order involves residence, custody, school, childcare, firearms, property retrieval or third-party contact, identify each term that may need review.

For respondents

For respondents, every page served, the hearing date, related criminal matters, custody or residence matters, communications and prior orders or pending cases may matter. Do not rely on informal contact, a text message, a third-party communication or an agreed change without reviewing the actual order and any later court order.

Hearing matters

A PFA hearing may involve focused testimony and exhibits. The petition, temporary order, proof of service, photos, messages, call logs, medical or police information, witness names and date-specific facts may all matter. Keep originals unchanged and preserve the full context of messages or images.

Custody and family-court overlap

PFA terms may affect custody exchanges, school communication, household access, transportation and related family-court orders. Identify any current custody, support or divorce order and any upcoming conference, hearing, exchange matter or safety concern tied to the same family matter.

Criminal or contempt overlap

If there is an arrest, criminal complaint, bail condition, probation matter, firearm-related term or alleged violation, say so. PFA, contempt and criminal cases can move on separate tracks, and the first discussion should identify known court dates, docket numbers and conditions already in place.

First-call information

Have the county, hearing date, order type, service status, involved party names, related docket numbers and any urgent safety, custody, residence or criminal-case matter ready. Keep confidential facts out of the initial message until we confirm that it can speak with you about the matter.

Protection from abuse

A PFA order changes life immediately.

A temporary PFA can remove someone from a home, restrict contact and affect firearms before a final hearing occurs.

James's view: An agreed order without findings of abuse can be a practical outcome, but it must be understood before anyone signs.

What controls the next step

Pennsylvania PFA cases arise under 23 Pa.C.S.A. § 6101 et seq.; firearm relinquishment can follow under 23 Pa.C.S.A. § 6108(a)(7).

Emergency, temporary and final PFA proceedings may involve different forums and different levels of proof.
