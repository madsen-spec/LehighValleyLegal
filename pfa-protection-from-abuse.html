For legal papers, hearing dates, service papers, alleged violations, or related criminal or custody questions, contact the office directly.

A useful first discussion starts with the exact order and petition, not a general description of the dispute. The wording of the order can affect contact, residence, custody exchange, school or childcare communication, property access, firearms restrictions, hearing preparation and what conduct might create a separate contempt or criminal issue.

Documents to have ready

  • temporary or final PFA orders, petitions, and hearing notices;
  • service papers, police reports, criminal complaints, or bail documents if any exist;
  • custody orders, parenting schedules, school or medical documents involving children;
  • messages, photos, call logs, medical records, witness names, and dated timelines;
  • any firearm, residence, property, employment, school, childcare, exchange-location or third-party-contact terms listed in the order;
  • any violation, contempt, arrest, bail, probation or criminal-case documents connected to the same events or parties.

Call-first situations

Contact the office when there is a hearing date, arrest, contempt allegation, custody overlap, firearm-related term, or uncertainty about what an order allows or prohibits. Do not rely on a short message alone for urgent PFA matters.

For petitioners

Bring the order, petition, hearing notice, service information, police or medical records, relevant communications, child-related records, and a timeline of the events the court has been asked to review. If the order involves residence, custody, school, childcare, firearms, property retrieval or third-party contact, identify each term that may need explanation or enforcement.

For respondents

Bring every page served, the hearing date, any related criminal documents, custody or residence documents, communication records, and information about prior orders or pending cases. Do not assume that informal contact, a text message, a third-party communication or an agreed change is safe without reviewing the actual order and any later court documents.

Hearing preparation

A PFA hearing may require focused testimony and organized exhibits. Bring the petition, the temporary order, proof of service, photos, messages, call logs, medical or police records, witness names and any records that show dates, locations, contact attempts or child-related issues. Keep originals unchanged and make sure the attorneys can see the full context of messages or images.

Custody and family-court overlap

PFA terms may affect custody exchanges, school communication, household access, transportation and related family-court orders. Bring current custody, support or divorce orders and identify any upcoming conference, hearing, exchange issue or safety concern tied to the same family matter.

Criminal or contempt overlap

If there is an arrest, criminal complaint, bail condition, probation issue, firearm-related term or alleged violation, bring those papers too. PFA, contempt and criminal cases can move on separate tracks, and the first discussion should identify every court date, judge, docket number and condition already in place.

First-call information

Have the county, hearing date, order type, service status, involved party names, related docket numbers and any urgent safety, custody, residence or criminal-case issue ready. Keep confidential facts out of the initial message until the office confirms that it can speak with you about the issue.

Important checkpoints

Check the paper, date and forum before deciding what to do next.
