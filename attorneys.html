Bio

A local practice with courtroom roots.

James P. Madsen, Esquire was born in Flemington, New Jersey and has spent most of his adult life in Easton, Pennsylvania. Through Lehigh Valley Legal, he provides a direct first point of contact for Pennsylvania matters.

Mr. Madsen earned a B.A. in Political Science from The American University in Washington, D.C., an A.A. from Raritan Valley Community College, and a J.D. from Drexel University Thomas R. Kline School of Law.

Before and during private practice, he worked in prosecution, public defense and legal services for people who could not afford counsel. He opened a private practice in 2012.

His trial experience includes matters in Berks, Lehigh, Monroe, Northampton and Philadelphia Counties. He has argued before the Pennsylvania Superior Court and Pennsylvania Supreme Court.

His published and teaching work includes the CLE seminar publication Representing Parents with Pending Criminal Cases and published commentary on Commonwealth v. Chickin and Commonwealth v. McClelland.

Practice focus

What this practice is built to handle.

Lehigh Valley Legal focuses on Pennsylvania matters where local procedure, records and judgment matter: criminal defense, DUI, firearms, family law, protection-from-abuse, employment, personal injury, estate planning, land use and civil disputes.

Not every matter is a fit. Medical malpractice, securities matters, class actions and highly specialized federal matters may need referral or different counsel.

Why this practice

Clients deserve straight answers before big decisions.

People usually call after something has already gone wrong: an arrest, a court order, a denial, a family conflict, an insurance letter or a deadline. The first job is to slow the problem down enough to see what is actually in front of us.

I would rather tell a caller that a matter is not a fit than dress up uncertainty as confidence. Good legal work usually includes the record, the forum and the next decision that has to be made.
