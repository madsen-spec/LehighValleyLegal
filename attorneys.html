What to expect

The first conversation should be practical.

Most legal issues start with a paper, notice, charge, order, letter, agency message or date. When contacting the office, identify what arrived, where the matter is pending, who is involved and whether any hearing, appeal, filing or response date is scheduled.

Do not send confidential details until the office confirms that conflicts have been checked and the matter can be discussed.

Useful first-contact details

  • County, court, agency or board involved
  • Next court date, hearing, appeal or response deadline
  • Names of involved parties for conflict check
  • The newest notice, order, citation, complaint or letter
