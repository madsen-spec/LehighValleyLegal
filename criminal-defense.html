Defense work

Early decisions can shape the case.

Criminal cases in Pennsylvania often involve an alleged offense, police investigation, arrest, citation or criminal complaint. Early court events may include preliminary arraignment, bail conditions, a preliminary hearing or a scheduling order in the Court of Common Pleas. Those early events can shape the case even before formal discovery is complete.

A preliminary hearing may be the first meaningful chance to test the allegations, preserve testimony, address bail or make a strategic decision about how the case should move forward.

Before speaking with police, waiving a hearing, entering a plea or missing a court date, it is important to understand what the Commonwealth must prove, what information is missing and what practical options may be available.

A strong first defense conversation is practical: read the charges, identify the court date, check bail conditions, preserve urgent issues and decide what needs attention before the next appearance.

Local court posture

Defense work depends on the record.

In Easton-area criminal cases, the early questions are concrete: what are the charges, which court is handling the case, what bail conditions apply, what did police say or do, and what could happen if the next court date is missed.

If the case is already in the Northampton County Court of Common Pleas, the docket number, scheduling orders, plea documents, trial notices, sentencing information and probation status may matter. If the case is still before a magisterial district judge, the complaint, summons, affidavit if available, bail terms and preliminary hearing notice help show where things stand.

If there is a warrant, detainer or missed court date, do not assume the issue will resolve itself. The first question is procedural: which court issued the warrant, why it was issued, whether bail was forfeited or revoked, and what document explains the next step.

Preliminary hearings

The preliminary hearing is often the first major court event in a Pennsylvania criminal case. The defense may need to consider witness issues, bail concerns, discovery, cross-examination, negotiation, waiver or a contested hearing based on the facts and client goals.

Warrants, bail and detainers

When a warrant, bail issue, probation detainer or missed hearing is involved, the first question is which court issued it, what triggered it and what step is needed to get back before the right judge.

Negotiation, trial and sentencing choices

Resolution may involve diversion, reduced charges, plea negotiations, trial or sentencing advocacy. The right path depends on the record, evidence, client goals and practical risk.

Collateral consequences

Criminal allegations can affect employment, licenses, education, immigration, custody, housing, military status and firearm rights. Those concerns should be raised early so they are considered before bail, plea, hearing and sentencing decisions are made.

Easton criminal defense FAQ

Questions people ask before contacting the office.

Should I talk to police if they say they only want my side?

Call an attorney before giving a statement. A conversation that seems informal may still affect an investigation, charging decision, bail issue or later testimony. If police are asking for a statement, identify the agency, officer, alleged incident and whether charges have already been filed.

What matters for a preliminary hearing?

The complaint, affidavit if available, summons, bail terms, hearing notice, witness information, messages, photos, video and anything showing your location or communications may matter. If you received court mail, keep the envelope too.

Can a criminal case create work or licensing issues?

Yes. Employment, professional licensing, school, firearm, immigration, housing and family-law consequences can matter before a final disposition. Raise those concerns during the first conversation so they are not treated as an afterthought.

What if the hearing is very soon?

Contact the office and identify the court, date, time, judge or magisterial district judge, docket number and charges. Do not assume a continuance will be granted.
