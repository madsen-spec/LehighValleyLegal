Consultation preparation

Start with what brought you here.

Most calls are easier when the latest notice or letter is nearby: a court notice, criminal complaint, custody order, PFA hearing notice, PennDOT letter, unemployment determination, agency notice, insurance request, contract demand or lawsuit paper.

Add a short timeline with names, dates and copies of the key documents. For family, criminal, employment, injury and civil issues, a few organized facts are more useful than a long unsorted history.

If the paper says you must appear, answer, appeal, respond or file by a date, contact the office with that paper in front of you.

What the guides can do

The guides can help you sort documents, spot the next date, list the people involved and prepare a short timeline. They cannot decide legal strategy or replace advice based on the actual papers and facts.

Call before you keep reading

Do not wait to finish a checklist if a court date, appeal date, arrest, warrant, PFA hearing, PennDOT notice, unemployment hearing, lawsuit paper, insurance date, agency filing or request for a signed statement is already in front of you.

Have the notice, letter, message, dashboard message or court paper nearby when contacting the office.

Working packet

A useful packet can be simple.

You do not need a perfect file before contacting the office. A small, clear packet is usually better than waiting to build a perfect file.

  • Put the newest dated notice or court paper at the front.
  • Sort documents by date.
  • Keep envelopes and cover letters when they show mailing or receipt dates.
  • Label photos with the date, location, and what they show.
  • Save text messages, messages, portal messages, and letters with visible dates.
  • Separate court filings, agency notices, medical documents, financial information, and correspondence.
  • Make a one-page timeline before adding long background notes.

Do not alter, delete, redact, annotate, or mark up originals. Keep originals separate and use copies for discussion.

Dates can come from different places

A date may depend on the court, agency, claim type, service date, mailing date, event date or words in the notice. If more than one date appears, write each one down separately.

Keep the actual notice, envelope, message, dashboard message or court paper. General online summaries cannot confirm what your specific paper requires.

  • County, court, agency, claim number and case number if known
  • Next hearing, response date or other listed date
  • Names of involved parties and witnesses
  • Copies of notices, orders, letters, messages and agency communications

Criminal or DUI matters

Bring citations, complaints, bail papers, summonses, warrants, hearing notices, PennDOT letters, chemical-test documents, refusal documents, ARD papers, and any messages, photos, or videos. Identify the county, arresting agency, court, next date, and license-related notices.

Family law and custody issues

Bring current orders, petitions, conference notices, parenting schedules, school records, medical records, childcare records, income documents, expense records, messages, and proposed schedule ideas. Identify any PFA, relocation, contempt, support, custody, or divorce-related deadline.

Employment and unemployment issues

Bring termination letters, disciplinary records, handbooks, schedules, pay stubs, HR messages, unemployment determinations, hearing notices, agency documents, witness names, and a dated timeline. Identify the employer, work location, separation date, agency, and next date.

Personal injury issues

Bring photographs, reports, medical documents, bills, insurance letters, claim numbers, wage-loss records, repair estimates, and any request for a statement, authorization, release, or recorded interview.

Land use and civil issues

Bring contracts, leases, deeds, notices, permits, photographs, maps, invoices, estimates, correspondence, board papers, municipal notices, and any complaint, summons, hearing notice, demand letter, or appeal instruction.
