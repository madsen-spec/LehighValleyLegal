Before contacting the office

Focus on what brought you here.

If a court notice, criminal complaint, custody order, PFA hearing notice, PennDOT letter, unemployment determination, agency notice, insurance request, contract demand or lawsuit has arrived, use it to understand what is happening and whether a date is listed.

A short timeline can help, especially when several events happened close together. For family, criminal, employment, injury and civil issues, the important point is what changed and what needs attention now.

If something requires you to appear, answer, appeal, respond or file by a date, do not wait on a checklist.

Call before you keep reading

Keep the notice, letter, message, dashboard message or court filing nearby when contacting the office.

Key information

You do not need a perfect file to ask for help.

A short summary, the main notice or order, and the date you are worried about can be enough to start. More detail can come later if the office confirms the issue can be discussed.

  • Put the most recent notice or court filing at the front.
  • Sort documents by date.
  • Keep envelopes and cover letters when they show mailing or receipt dates.
  • Label photos with the date, location, and what they show.
  • Save text messages, messages, portal messages, and letters with visible dates.
  • Separate court filings, agency notices, medical documents, financial information, and correspondence.
  • Make a one-page timeline before adding long background notes.

Do not alter, delete, redact, annotate, or mark up originals. Keep originals separate and use copies for discussion.

Dates can come from different places

A date may depend on the court, agency, claim type, service date, mailing date, event date or words in the notice. If more than one date appears, write each one down separately.

Keep the actual notice, envelope, message, dashboard message or court filing. General online summaries cannot confirm what your specific situation requires.

  • County, court, agency, claim number and case number if known
  • Next hearing, response date or other listed date
  • Names of involved parties and witnesses
  • Copies of notices, orders, letters, messages and agency communications

Criminal or DUI matters

For criminal or DUI matters, citations, complaints, bail terms, warrants, hearing notices, PennDOT letters, testing or refusal issues, ARD status, messages, photos or videos may matter. Identify the county, arresting agency, court, upcoming date and license issue.

Family law and custody issues

For family-law and custody issues, current orders, petitions, conference notices, parenting schedules, school or medical information, childcare issues, income, expenses and messages may matter. Identify any PFA, relocation, contempt, support, custody or divorce-related deadline.

Employment and unemployment issues

For employment and unemployment issues, termination letters, discipline, handbooks, schedules, pay information, HR messages, unemployment determinations, agency notices, witness names and a timeline may matter. Identify the employer, work location, separation date, agency and upcoming date.

Personal injury issues

For injury issues, photographs, reports, treatment information, bills, insurance letters, claim numbers, wage-loss information, repair estimates and requests for statements or releases may matter.

Land use and civil issues

For land-use and civil issues, contracts, leases, deeds, notices, permits, photographs, maps, invoices, estimates, correspondence, board decisions, municipal notices and any complaint, summons, demand letter or appeal instruction may matter.
