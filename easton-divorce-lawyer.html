Local preparation

Divorce planning should start with the papers and the timeline.

A divorce matter may involve court filings, service, property division, support, custody, insurance, business interests, retirement accounts and debt. The first useful step is identifying what has already happened and what date is next.

For Easton and Northampton County matters, local court procedures and scheduling can affect how quickly a response, conference or filing decision is needed. Organize the newest court notice, complaint, service papers and any related family-court orders before discussing strategy.

What matters first

What to gather before the first call.

Court

Filings and service

Bring the complaint, acceptance or affidavit of service, scheduling order, notices and any prior family-court orders.

Money

Income, assets and debt

Gather pay stubs, tax returns, account statements, retirement statements, deeds, titles, mortgage papers and credit-card statements.

Family

Custody and support overlap

Identify any custody, support, alimony, PFA, relocation or contempt issue that may affect the divorce timeline.

Next steps

A clear packet is better than a long unsorted history.

A short chronology can help, but the court documents and financial materials usually matter more than memory alone. Keep originals separate and use copies for discussion when possible.

Do not send confidential financial or family details in an initial message. Use the first contact to identify the case type, county, names for a conflict check and the next date.

Northampton County court materials

County divorce and family-court forms are local to the court. Confirm the filing packet, service papers, and next date against the actual Northampton County documents received.

Custody and support overlap

Divorce, custody, support, PFA, and contempt matters can move on separate tracks. Keep each order, petition, and notice separate by case type and date.

Questions

Common preparation questions.

What should I bring first?

Bring the divorce filing, service papers, court notices, current orders, income documents, tax returns and any papers showing the next date.

Can divorce involve custody or support at the same time?

Yes. Divorce, custody, support, alimony and PFA issues can overlap, but each may have its own procedure and timing.

Does this page give legal advice?

No. It provides general preparation information. Advice depends on the actual papers, facts and deadlines.
