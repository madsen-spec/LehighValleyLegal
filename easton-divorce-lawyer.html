Divorce planning

Divorce planning should be practical from the beginning.

A divorce matter may involve property division, support, custody, insurance, business interests, retirement accounts and debt. The legal work is easier to focus when the immediate household and parenting concerns are clear.

For Easton and Northampton County matters, local court procedure may affect response timing, conference scheduling or filing decisions. If anything has already been filed, the current court notice or complaint still matters, but it should not drown out the real family and financial problems that brought you here.

What happens next

The first discussion should separate urgent issues from background.

A short timeline can help, but the first priority is deciding what may need immediate attention: a filing deadline, a support issue, access to money, parenting time, housing or safety.

Do not send confidential financial or family details in an initial message. For scheduling, identify the county, the kind of case and the people involved.

Divorce questions

Divorce questions.

What should I talk about first?

Focus on what has changed: filing status, living arrangements, parenting time, support, access to money, property concerns and any scheduled court event.

Can divorce involve custody or support at the same time?

Yes. Divorce, custody, support, alimony and PFA issues can overlap, but each may have its own procedure and timing.

Does this page give legal advice?

No. It provides general information. Advice depends on the actual facts, filings and timing.
